Håkon Wium Lie


Web Pioneer / Standards Activist / Politician

In the near future, the web is going to be the master copy of human knowledge. We need to figure out ways to use that knowledge.


Håkon Wium Lie was born on July 16th, 1965 (age 55) and currently lives in Oslo, Norway. He is a Norwegian web pioneer, a standards activist, as of 2010 is the Chief Technology Officer of Opera Software, and even co-founded the Pirate Party of Norway! He attended Ostfold University College, West Georgia College, and MIT Media Lab. He received his Masters Degree in visual studies in 1991.


Håkon Wium Lie is best known for proposing the concept of Cascading Style Sheets, also known as CSS. After joining W3C in 1995, he worked on the CSS specifications, including CSS1 and CSS2. Over the next decade, CSS established itself as one of the fundamental web standards, having a great impact on the likes of typography, aesthetics, and even accessibility of the web.


Håkon (being a standards activist) has influenced many standards to be implemented into modern web browsers.


In 2005 he challenged Bill Gates, asking as to why Internet Explorer did not support common web standards, he then launched a tool to benchmark browser functionality in displaying aspects of HTML markup called “Acid2”.


In 2006 he campaigned for browsers to support downloadable web fonts using common font formats. As of 2011, this has now been adopted and implemented on all major browsers.


In 2007, he campaigned for the video element, making it easier to publish videos on the web. He presented the video element to be in combination with the WebM format.
